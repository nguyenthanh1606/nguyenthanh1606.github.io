8 points to remember when buying wine

From past to present, wine is not only a drink with sharp aroma and attractive taste but also a symbol of noble noble value. Here are eight things to keep in mind when choosing a bottle of wine that suits your meal or a luxurious gift.

 

1. Purpose of use

 

First, you must determine what is the purpose of your wine purchase? The bottle of wine on your hand is to enjoy with your family, give a large customer, give a relative or a party ... Moreover, the object that you want to give is connoisseur of wine or not is also a problem. If your target audience is a good wine connoisseur, it would be unsightly to offer cheap wine. In contrast, giving expensive wine to people who do not exprerience is quite wasteful.

 

2. Type of wine
Wine has many types: red wine, white wine, champagne, effervescent wine, rose wine, Chilean wine, ... and each type is suitable for each different purpose. On special occasions such as birthdays, inaugurations, weddings often use sparkling wine, or a bottle of premium Champagne to celebrate.

As for buying to drink, you should see the menu that day has nothing to choose the perfect wine. The easiest and best way is to remember that red wine will go with dishes made from red meat such as beafsteak, grilled lamb chops, ... And white wine will go along with bright dishes such as seafood, chicken, cheese, ...

 

3. Taste

 

If you try it, you should sip it a little before you buy. Each different wine made from different grape varieties possesses extremely rich flavors. White wine is made from yellow, green or red grapes, so it tastes sour and sweet from grape meat. Meanwhile, red wine has a bitter taste due to the grape skin being mixed with grape juice during tempering.

 

3. Place of production
Each famous wine producing region has its own distinctive products with its own flavor and name. In red wine, the wine in Chile is often stronger than the gentle French wine.

 

4. Year of printing on wine
In a year, there are 5 grape harvest seasons in each region. In any rainy season, the wine will be more delicious. If you are drinking wine numbered in 2009, it means that producers picked the 2009 vintage to make this bottle. Up to now, the bottle is 9 years old. Basically, bottles of wine aged 5-10 years will be of better quality, of course the price is also higher.

Before buying, you should check the label on the wine bottle for more information on the taste, production method, brewing time, alcohol content, importer, distributor and production area. Some good wines also have a certificate of the value of the wine, an international title that the brand is recognized.

 

7. Score printed on the bottle
In Europe or the US, consumers often buy alcohol based on the score indicated on the bottle. This score is rated by leading experts from major food journals, such as the famous wine critic Robert Parker of Wine Advocate. The scale is usually 80-100. Under 80 does not qualify for grading a bottle of fine wine. If over 90 points will be good wine.

 

8. Genuine distributor and importer
Choosing a genuine distributor and importer, a prestigious wine selling location is an important consideration when you decide to buy wine. With no small price, "genuine" wine also helps you feel secure when drinking or making luxurious gifts.

 

Please remember these 8 points to choose a bottle of premium wine that best suits you!
